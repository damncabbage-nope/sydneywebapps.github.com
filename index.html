Security: Protect your Web Apps

Wednesday, 13 March 2013 at 6:30pm
Location TBA

We've all seen it in the movies, criminals weedling their way into some computer system and creating havoc. But what's the real story? How do the sites we use everyday protect us from having our data stolen?

Find out why should you care about web security, what mistakes you could be making right now, and leave the meetup knowing how safe you really are.

Speakers

Guided Spinlocks for Fun and Profit

Johnny Come Lately

When a thread arrives, it atomically obtains and then increments the queue ticket. It then atomically compares its ticket with the dequeue ticket. If they are the same, the thread is permitted to enter the critical section.

Rails' Key under the Mat

Johnny Come Lately

Open-source projects all too commonly come with too many batteries included. This talk will show you what to look out for when grabbing an open source Rails app off GitHub for your own use, and what you risk if you don't.
